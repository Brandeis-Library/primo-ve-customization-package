What is OneSearch? What does it do?

OneSearch provides simple, one-stop searching for all of the library's physical collections as well as many of our online collections. It's also a portal to manage your research and your library account. Start typing in the search box to view search scope options.

  • Library Catalog: Use this option to search for books and ebooks; streaming video and DVDs; and journals by title. Article-level information for journals is not visible at this level of searching.
  • Articles & More: Use this option when you are looking for articles from scholarly journals, newspapers, magazines, encyclopedias, and dictionaries.
  • Course Reserves: Use this option to search for books and films that have been set aside for your courses.
  • Archives and Special Collections: Use this option to search for all Archives and Special Collections materials. This includes books and archival collections (both physical and digital).
  • Brandeis ScholarWorks: Use this option to search for research and creative scholarship created by Brandeis faculty, students, and staff. This includes papers, theses, dissertations, books, reports, interviews, data, and multimedia.
  • All OneSearch: Use this option to search all of the above.

Although OneSearch results can include materials that the Library doesn't own, you can request them through Interlibrary Loan.

For more information, see our FAQ.
